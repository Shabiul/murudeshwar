Journey Into Diving — Discovery Through Adventure
Sarith grew up loving adventure. Hiking, exploring forests, trying anything that pushed his limits — these were early habits. But the ocean was always the biggest mystery.

When he completed his first scuba course, everything changed.

Q&A
Q: What made you choose diving as a career?

"Adventure always called me, but the underwater world felt different. It wasn't just adrenaline… it felt like purpose. The moment I descended for the first time, I knew this is where I belong."

Training & Growth — Building a Technical Mindset
As he moved through his certifications (OW, AOW, EFR, Rescue, and Dive Master), instructors noticed something unique — Sarith had a natural ability to understand underwater behavior: currents, fish movement, diver psychology, and spatial awareness.

He practiced buoyancy for hours, repeating drills until movement became instinctive. Today, his neutral buoyancy is one of the best in the team.

Experience With Large Marine Animals — Learning Respect
Sarith has interacted with lemon sharks, blacktip sharks, manta rays, turtles, large schooling jacks, massive stingrays. These encounters shaped him professionally and personally.

Q&A
Q: How do you stay calm around big animals?

"Fear comes from misunderstanding. When you study behavior, you know how to position yourself and respect their space. They are not threats — they are teachers."

Most Memorable Dive — Feeding a Lemon Shark
Hand-feeding a Lemon Shark is the dive Sarith always talks about. The shark approached slowly, gliding with quiet confidence. He extended the food, the shark took it gently, and swam away.

"It was powerful… humbling. For a moment, everything felt connected — respect going both ways."
