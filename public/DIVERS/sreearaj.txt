Journey Into Diving
Manu's relationship with the sea began as childlike curiosity. His Open Water course in 2018 revealed the power of breath and stillness. Freediving taught him to read his body; scuba taught him to share that control with others.

Q&A
Q: Why freediving and scuba together?

"Freediving rewires the nervous system. When I teach scuba students breath control from freediving, their panic responses drop dramatically."

Training & Growth
Manu trained across disciplines: scuba specialties, structured freediving, and responsible spearfishing. He also studied local fish behavior and reef ecology so his guidance is rooted in both technique and natural history.

"Understanding fish behavior means better positioning, less disturbance, and safer encounters."

Most Memorable Dive
Bangaram, Lakshadweep stands out: clear water, vast coral gardens, and a sense of being in a pristine ecosystem.

"There's a hush in those waters — like the reef is holding its breath too."

Behind the Scenes — What Guests Don't See
Manu runs private breathwork sessions for nervous divers and those doing deep or prolonged dives. He also volunteers with local coral monitoring projects and introduces interested guests to citizen-science tasks while on trips.

"Even a simple photo of a coral outplant gives someone a stake in the reef's future."

Team Experience at Scuba Spirit
He's a quiet mentor: demonstrating a technique, then letting the student try with close oversight. The team trusts him for complex dive planning and deep-reef behaviour interpretation.
